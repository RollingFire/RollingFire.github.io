This Website

I figured that a website would be an excellent way to show my portfolio and market myself to future employers along with gaining experience in writing HTML and CSS.

Key Words

  • HTML5
  • CSS3
  • Responsiveness
  • Web Design

Things Learned

  • Attention to detail

Successes

  • Didn’t compromise on desired outcome

Challenges

  • Coming up with the design
  • Small adjustments of formatting based on changes

Since most of my programing experience is self-taught, I was searching for a good way to market myself, my experience, and my skills. A resume fails when it comes to showing what you can actually do. Talk is cheap, and actions speak louder than words. So, I wanted to find a way to show my work. GitHub is a good place to post my work for people to go see, but not a good way to draw people to see it. That is where this website comes into play. This website fills in where a resume doesn’t and is a better way to represent my personality as well as my work than what a resume could do.

When I started this project, I didn’t know HTML or CSS, but the most difficult part of this project was coming up with a design and layout. I work best when I have a direction or some sort of restraint, restriction, or benchmark to achieve. When I don’t have those, I tend to create them so that my best work comes out.

To get started on this website, I looked around at other people’s personal websites for inspiration. I took the bits and pieces that I gathered from others and tried to make it my own by infused it with my style and personality. I know there are places where the design could be improved, but I am a software engineer not a designer. At the end of the day, I am happy with the outcome. If I wasn’t happy with it, you wouldn’t be seeing it. At times I can be a perfectionist, but I understand that there will always be something that could be improved. At some point, you must step back and give yourself the permission to stop and call it a job well done so that you can move on to the next thing.
